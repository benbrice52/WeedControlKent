Child-Safe Herbicides for Educational Facilities

We use only approved herbicides suitable for application in areas accessed by children and young people:

Approved Products for School Use

  • Glyphosate-based herbicides: Non-selective total weed control for hard surfaces, approved for educational use with appropriate re-entry times
  • Residual herbicides: Long-lasting protection applied during holidays to reduce frequency of treatments
  • Selective herbicides: For weed control in grass areas and sports pitches without damaging turf
  • Moss treatments: Specific products for playground and path safety, particularly important in shaded areas

Safety Standards We Follow

  • HSE-approved products only: All herbicides comply with Health & Safety Executive regulations for use near children
  • Manufacturer guidelines: Strict adherence to recommended re-entry times (typically 48-72 hours for school areas)
  • Weather-dependent application: Treatments only applied in suitable conditions to ensure safety and effectiveness
  • Dilution rates: Precise mixing to manufacturer specifications for optimal safety and efficacy

Application Methods for Schools

  • Controlled boom spraying: Efficient coverage of large areas like car parks and sports pitch surrounds
  • Knapsack precision application: Targeted treatment for pathways, edges, and sensitive areas
  • Weed wiper technology: Contact-only application for areas near planted zones, minimizing drift risk
  • Spot treatment capability: Minimal application for small areas or mid-term emergency weed control

PA1 & PA6 AW Certification: Why It Matters for Schools

PA1 (Safe Use of Pesticides): Foundation qualification proving operators understand safe pesticide handling, storage, and application including legal responsibilities.

PA6 AW (Aquatic Weed Control): Specialist certification for herbicide application near water features, drainage channels, and ponds ‚Äì critical for schools with water features or drainage systems.

These certifications ensure your contractor has the legal qualifications and technical knowledge to treat school grounds safely and in compliance with UK pesticide regulations.

Common School Weed Control Questions

When should we schedule our annual school weed control treatments?

The optimal schedule is: Easter holidays (pre-season preventative), summer holidays (comprehensive treatment), October half-term (top-up), and Christmas holidays (winter weeds/moss). This four-treatment approach provides year-round weed-free grounds.

How long before pupils can access treated areas?

Standard re-entry time for school herbicide applications is 48-72 hours after treatment, depending on product used and weather conditions. We always schedule treatments early in holidays to ensure complete drying before term starts. For weekend treatments, Friday afternoon application allows safe Monday access.

Are the herbicides safe for use around children?

Yes. We use only HSE-approved herbicides rated for use in areas accessed by children, applied by PA1/PA6 certified operators. When proper re-entry times are observed (48-72 hours), treated areas are completely safe. Full COSHH assessments and product safety data are provided for your records.

Do you provide documentation for Ofsted or local authority inspections?

Yes. We provide comprehensive documentation including risk assessments, method statements, COSHH assessments, treatment records, insurance certificates, and operator qualifications. All documentation is audit-ready and meets H&S inspection requirements.

Can you treat our school grounds during term time?

Yes, for urgent requirements or low-access areas like car parks and perimeters. We implement additional safety measures including temporary fencing, signage, and coordination with school staff. However, holiday treatments are recommended for playground and sports areas where possible.

What's included in an annual school weed control contract?

Contracts typically include 4 scheduled treatments (Easter, summer, October, Christmas), comprehensive site coverage, all documentation and risk assessments, priority holiday scheduling, emergency callout availability, and fixed annual pricing with no hidden costs.

How much does professional school weed control cost?

Costs vary by site size and complexity. Typical annual contracts range from ¬£900-¬£1,500 for small primary schools, ¬£1,500-¬£2,500 for medium primary schools, ¬£2,500-¬£3,800 for large primary schools, and ¬£3,800-¬£11,000 for secondary schools. All quotes include professional service, qualified operators, insurance, and documentation.

Do you offer discounts for multi-academy trusts (MATs)?

Yes. MATs managing multiple schools receive bulk pricing discounts, coordinated scheduling across all sites, centralized invoicing, and consistent service standards. Contact us for multi-site pricing.

What qualifications do your operators have?

All operators hold PA1 (Safe Use of Pesticides) certification as legal minimum. For work near school ponds, drainage channels, or water features, we deploy PA6 AW certified specialists. We're also BALI accredited, demonstrating commitment to professional industry standards.

Can you treat our school car park separately from playground areas?

Yes. We can treat different areas on different schedules. Car parks can often be treated during term time (weekend application) while playground and sports areas are reserved for holiday treatments. This flexibility helps schools manage urgent areas without waiting for full holiday periods.
